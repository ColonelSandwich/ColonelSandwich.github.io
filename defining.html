On the topic of "Pseudo 3D"


While discussing sprite based 3D and Super Scalers, one is bound to come across the term "Pseudo 3D." I'm not about to get into specifics of why I think it's a silly term because we'd likely be here all day, but generally I think "Pseudo 3D" best applies to faking real 3D calculations behind smoke and mirrors. Some games fake these calculations entirely while some other may only fake it for things such as curves in roads or a scrolling floor.



Some people however, use it to describe sprite based 3D in its entirety, which isn't actually always true! So let's define what the process of real 3D perspective projection calculations are and aren't, and maybe go over some examples in other games while we're at it!



Perspective Projection: A Window into the Game's World!


To start with, imagine we have a world of sorts, inside of the game. Objects inside the game world have x, y, and z coordinates (but for now we'll only worry about x and y)

Here's a basic world with a couple of trees in it, each with their own X and Y coordinates! (I apologize for the crudeness of the tree drawing, except I DON'T apologize because you can live with it)


Now, the game world is vast and expansive beyond our wildest imagination, but we, the player, need some way to be able to see into this incredible world! Imagine, if you will, that a window of some sort could allow us to peer into this game world and see the majesty of the field of poorly drawn trees...


Aha! Now what do we have here?


At the bottom of the screen, there is a new area with a circle in front of a light blue line. This circle represents "us" as the viewer and the light blue line represents a "view window" of sorts that will allow us to see into the game's world! On the next page, I will explain how we take these points in the game's world and project them onto our view window to make a convincing 3D effect out of these HORRIBLE sprites. Let's go!!
